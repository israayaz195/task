Al-Aqsa Mosque

Al-Aqsa Mosque, located in the Old City of Jerusalem, is one of the holiest sites in Islam. It is part of the larger Al-Haram Al-Sharif (The Noble Sanctuary) complex, which also includes the Dome of the Rock. The mosque holds deep religious significance, as it was the first Qibla (direction of prayer) for Muslims and the site of the Prophet Muhammad's miraculous night journey, Al-Isra and Miraj. Al-Aqsa is a symbol of faith, history, and heritage for Muslims around the world.

Israa and Mi'raj

The Isra and Miraj is a significant event in Islam where the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) was miraculously transported from Mecca to Jerusalem and then ascended to the heavens. This journey affirmed his prophethood and established the five daily prayers. It represents a profound spiritual experience and connection with Allah.
